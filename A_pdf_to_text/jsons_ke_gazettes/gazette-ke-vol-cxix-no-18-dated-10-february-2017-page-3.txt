4. For the purposes of performing its functions, the Steering Committee— 
(a) shall regulate its own procedures; 
(b) shall hold quarterly meetings or special meetings as convened by the Chairperson at such places as the Steering Committee shall consider desirable;and 
(c) may co-opt members or appoint nominees with necessary expertise as it considers necessary. 
5. There shall be the Ushanga Kenya Secretariat responsible for the day to day running of the Ushanga Kenya Initiative and it shall comprise— 
(a) a co-ordinator who shall head the secretariat; and 
(b) such other persons as may be deemed necessary by the steering committee for the performance of the functions of the secretariat. 
6. The terms of reference of the Ushanga Kenya Secretariat are to— 
(a) undertake a mapping and profiling of the status of the bead industry in Kenya and more specifically, in the target counties of Baringo, Kajiado, Marsabit, Narok, Samburu, Turkana and West Pokot; 
(b) promote entrepreneurship and commercialization in the bead industry through the training and capacity building of women from the pastoral communities in the target counties; 
(c) strengthen the bargaining power of women from the pastoral communities in the targeted counties through the promotion of product quality, value addition and marketing; 
(d) raise awareness on the cultural and economic value of the bead especially in critical supporting sectors such as hospitality, industry, industrialization, labour and technical education; 
(e) support and promote the marketing and visibility of bead products through the exploration of markets and other visibility opportunities; 
(f) collaborate with all relevant stakeholders and promote their active participation on the Ushanga Initiative; 
(g) report on quarterly basis and seek overall vision and policy guidance from the Steering Committee; and 
(h) perform any other tasks and initiatives as requested by the Steering Committee. 
7. The Ushanga Initiative shall be financed through funds— 
(a) appropriated for it from the Ministry of Tourism and the target counties; and 
(b) received as grants, gifts or donations from well-wishers, sponsors and other development partners. 
8. The term of office of the Committee shall be a period of one year from the day of appointment and may thereafter be renewed annually, or as the Cabinet Secretary for Tourism may deem fit. 
9. The Secretariat of the Committee shall be based at the Ministry of Tourism Headquarters.
